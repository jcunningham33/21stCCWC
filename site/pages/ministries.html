Young Adult Ministry

The Bridge

Where Faith Meets Real Life

The Bridge is the young adult ministry of 21st Century Christian Worship Center, created for those seeking a deeper relationship with God through authentic community, biblical discussion, and spiritual growth.

Our gatherings provide a welcoming environment where young adults can ask honest questions, explore God's Word, and encourage one another through every season of life. Each session is centered on relevant topics, open discussion, practical application, and building meaningful relationships with Christ and with one another.

Whether you're new to your faith or have been walking with Christ for years, The Bridge is a place to grow, belong, and be strengthened.

"Bridging the gap between where you are and where God is calling you to be."

Meeting Time

Thursdays · 6:00 PM

What to Expect

  • Biblical discussion
  • Interactive Q&A
  • Real-life application
  • Community and fellowship
  • A welcoming environment for young adults
Marriage Ministry

Marriage Matters

Building Stronger Marriages. Centered on Christ.

Marriage Matters is a monthly ministry designed to encourage, equip, and strengthen marriages through biblical wisdom, practical teaching, and authentic conversations.

Each gathering features experienced speakers and couples who share real-life stories, biblical principles, and practical insights for building healthy, Christ-centered marriages. Every session concludes with an open Q&A, giving attendees the opportunity to engage in meaningful conversation and receive practical guidance for everyday life.

Whether you're newly married or have shared decades together, Marriage Matters offers a welcoming space to invest in your relationship and grow together in Christ.

"What therefore God hath joined together, let not man put asunder."
— Mark 10:9 (KJV)

Meeting Time

Fourth Saturday of Every Month · 6:00 PM

What to Expect

  • Inspiring guest speakers
  • Biblical teaching
  • Real-life marriage experiences
  • Live audience Q&A
  • Fellowship and community
